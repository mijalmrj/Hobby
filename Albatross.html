Albatross

Albatross is a renowned rock band from Nepal, known for their significant contributions to the Nepali music scene. Formed in 1998, the band has become a household name in Nepal and has gained a loyal following both within the country and among Nepali diaspora around the world.
The band's lineup has seen some changes over the years, but its core members have remained consistent. The founding members include vocalist and guitarist Sunny Tuladhar, guitarist Bhanu A, and bassist Surendra R Singh. They are often joined by other talented musicians for live performances and studio recordings.
Albatross is celebrated for their unique blend of rock music with Nepali folk elements and traditional instruments. Their music often incorporates elements of blues, grunge, and alternative rock, creating a distinct sound that resonates with audiences of all ages. Their lyrics often reflect on social issues, personal experiences, and the cultural identity of Nepal.
One of the band's most notable achievements is their contribution to the popularization of rock music in Nepal. They have released several successful albums, including "Hi:Fly" and "Ma Ra Malai," which have received critical acclaim and commercial success. Their songs, such as "Ful Ko Aankha Ma" and "Sambodhan," have become anthems for Nepali rock music enthusiasts.
In addition to their musical endeavors, Albatross has been actively involved in promoting and supporting the Nepali music industry. They have participated in numerous concerts, music festivals, and charity events, using their platform to raise awareness about social issues and advocate for positive change in Nepali society.
Overall, Albatross remains one of the most influential and respected bands in Nepal, with a legacy that spans over two decades. Their passion for music, dedication to their craft, and commitment to their roots have solidified their place as cultural icons in the Nepali music scene.
